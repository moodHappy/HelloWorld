I jolted awake on the tiny kindergarten nap mat, my 35-year-old mind trapped in this five-year-old body. "Holy crap," I muttered, then covered my mouth when Teacher Lisa glared at me.

While my classmates napped, I pondered my situation. Was this a second chance? Or maybe that experimental memory upload I volunteered for went haywire. Either way, I had adult knowledge, tiny hands, and absolutely no idea how to explain why I knew the stock market would crash in 2028.

I whispered to little Timmy beside me, "Dude, we need to build a time machine before snack time."
