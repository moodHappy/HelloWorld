I'd been deep undercover for over a year, carefully building trust within the gang. Every move calculated, every word measured. Yet somehow, they'd discovered my true identity. Now I hung suspended, ropes biting into my wrists.

"How did you find out?" I asked, my mind racing through my meticulous cover story.

The boss lit a cigarette, smirking. "Remember when I sent you to handle our social security payments? The office called back. They said our records showed you as a police officer, not a gang member. Your pension contributions gave you away, detective."
