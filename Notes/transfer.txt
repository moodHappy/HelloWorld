I'd been undercover in the Dragon Triad for 13 months, perfecting my gangster persona. Yet here I was, dangling from the warehouse rafters like a piñata.

"How'd you find out?" I wheezed.

The boss lit a cigar. "We were suspicious when you refused to help with tax fraud. Then I paid your social security."

"And?" I asked, spinning slowly.

"The clerk said, 'Officer Chen, your department's already covering this.' Then she asked if you wanted the receipt emailed to the station." He chuckled. "You cops and your benefits!"

I groaned. Of all the things to bust me—paperwork.
